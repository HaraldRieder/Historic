Mad Harry's MIDI Enhancer

2.8 The Velocities Window

Here you can modify the note-on velocities of individual notes, i.e. how the note-on velocity of a note received on the MIDI input of the computer is transformed before it is sent to the MIDI output. The note-on velocity usually controls the volume an/or cutoff frequency of a sound. Purpose: correction of not 100% balanced keyboards and adaptation of keyboards to expanders.

  • "linear" maps a note-on velocity on the inputs linear to the outputs

  • "quadratic" maps quadratically. I.e. you have to press the keys more hard for higher note-on velocity values on the outputs.

  • "fix minimum" means that a velocity of 0 on an input channel will always be mapped to a velocity of 0 on an output channel.

  • "fix maximum" means that a velocity of 127 on an input channel will always be mapped to a velocity of 127 on an output channel.

The slope of the mapping function can be adjusted per note with the "-" and "+" buttons. All notes together can be selected/deselected with the "all" button.

Press "Test" to test your volumes mappings. Press any key (on the computer keyboard) to exit test mode.
